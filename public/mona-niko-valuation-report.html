01 — Executive Summary

Platform Overview

The Mona Niko Gallery digital platform is a purpose-built, full-stack web application that serves as the primary digital storefront, customer relationship hub, and operational backbone for a fine art gallery and studio in Mission Viejo, California. The platform was engineered from the ground up using modern enterprise web technologies — Next.js 16, Prisma ORM, Stripe payments, Cloudinary CDN, and Resend email — replacing a previous WordPress / WooCommerce installation that lacked the customization and operational depth required for a growing multi-channel art business.

The platform currently supports seven complete operational phases including e-commerce, marketing automation, customer accounts, commissions management, auctions, and a full admin dashboard. It is equivalent in functional scope to platforms typically costing $80,000–$180,000 to build with an agency.

05 — Development Phases

What Was Built — Phase by Phase

1

Phase 1 — Core E-Commerce & Payments

Stripe PaymentIntent API, Stripe webhook (order-to-DB + email), two-step checkout flow, /checkout/success page, Cloudinary image uploads, and all transactional emails via Resend (order confirmation, inquiry notification, workshop booking).

2

Phase 2 — Accounting & Finance

Expense and Invoice database models, P&L summary API, expenses CRUD, professional invoicing with line items, and print-to-PDF functionality for invoices and certificates.

3

Phase 3 — Email Marketing Automation

EmailTemplate and EmailBlast database models, full email blast sending via Resend batch API (100/call chunking), campaign management, subscriber segmentation, and template editor.

4

Phase 4 — SEO & Public Website

All 18 public listing and static pages split into server page.tsx (metadata export) + client Content.tsx. Async DB-backed sitemap.xml, per-page JSON-LD structured data (VisualArtwork, Product, Event, BlogPosting, BreadcrumbList), and full OpenGraph coverage.

5

Phase 5 — Customer Accounts & Portal

Customer registration, login, logout, HMAC-SHA256 signed session cookies, order history, wishlist, profile management, and promo code validation integrated into checkout.

6

Phase 6 — Commissions, Certificates & Collector Club

8-stage commission pipeline, auto-numbered Certificates of Authenticity (MN-YYYY-NNNN), and three-tier Collector Club memberships (Patron / Connoisseur / Luminary) with benefits and admin KPI dashboard.

7

Phase 7 — Live Auction System

Auction and AuctionBid database models, admin auction creation and management, public bidding with real-time polling, countdown timers, reserve prices, bid increment enforcement, and automated winner/outbid email notifications.

08 — Return on Investment

Why This Platform Is a Strategic Asset

Financial Independence from Art Platforms

By owning the platform rather than renting space on Artsy, Saatchi, or similar platforms, the gallery retains 100% of sales revenue (minus Stripe's 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction). For a gallery generating $100,000/year in online sales, this saves approximately $35,000 in Saatchi commissions or $6,000–$24,000 in Artsy fees. Over 5 years, this represents $30,000–$175,000 in retained revenue.

Brand Authority & Collector Trust

A professional custom platform signals seriousness and permanence to collectors. The Certificates of Authenticity, Collector Club memberships, and commission pipeline communicate the gallery's operational sophistication — attributes that directly influence a collector's willingness to invest in higher-priced original works.

Data Ownership & CRM

All customer data, order history, inquiry records, and subscriber information is owned by the gallery in its own database. This is a significant asset — unlike SaaS platforms where your customer data is locked in a vendor's system, this platform allows full data export, analysis, and use for targeted marketing at any time.

Scalability Without Additional Cost

The platform can handle 10x the current product and artwork count without architectural changes. Adding new workshops, products, journal posts, or auction items requires zero developer time — all done through the admin interface. Future phases (artist marketplace, NFT certificates, international shipping) can be added to the existing codebase.
