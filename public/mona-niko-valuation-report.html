01 Executive Summary

Platform Overview

The Mona Niko Gallery digital platform is a purpose-built, full-stack web application serving as the primary digital storefront, customer relationship hub, and operational backbone for a fine art gallery and studio in Mission Viejo, California.

The platform was engineered from the ground up using modern enterprise web technologies — Next.js 16, Prisma ORM, Stripe payments, Cloudinary CDN, and Resend email — replacing a previous WordPress / WooCommerce installation that lacked the customization and operational depth required for a growing multi-channel art business.

The platform currently supports seven complete operational phases including e-commerce, marketing automation, customer accounts, commissions management, live auctions, and a full admin dashboard with contextual help. It is equivalent in functional scope to platforms typically costing $80,000–$180,000 to build with an agency.

Beyond replacement value, the platform directly enables revenue streams (art sales, workshops, auctions, commissions, merch) that would otherwise require multiple third-party platforms with combined fees of $500–$2,000+ per month and 20–40% platform commissions.

05 Development Timeline

Seven Phases of Development

The platform was built incrementally across seven well-defined phases, each adding a complete business capability layer.

Phase 1 — Core Platform & Commerce

Established the foundation: Next.js 16 architecture, Prisma database, Stripe payment processing, Cloudinary image hosting, transactional email via Resend, and admin authentication.

Next.js 16 Stripe Payments Prisma ORM Cloudinary Admin Auth

Phase 2 — Finance & Accounting

Built the full financial management layer including a P&L dashboard, expense logging with categories, professional invoicing with line items, and PDF export for all financial documents.

P&L Dashboard Expense Tracking Invoicing PDF Export

Phase 3 — Email Marketing

Implemented a complete email marketing system with drag-free template management, subscriber segmentation, one-click bulk sending via Resend batch API, and campaign performance tracking.

Email Templates Bulk Blast API Segmentation Resend Integration

Phase 4 — SEO & Metadata

Comprehensive SEO implementation across all 18 public pages: JSON-LD structured data (VisualArtwork, Product, Event, BlogPosting), dynamic sitemaps, robots.txt, and OpenGraph social sharing.

Phase 5 — Customer Accounts

Full customer authentication system using HMAC-SHA256 signed session cookies, with order history, wishlist management, profile editing, and promo code validation at checkout.

Phase 6 — Commissions, Certificates & Collector Club

Conclusion

The Mona Niko Gallery platform represents a rare combination of deep technical sophistication and laser-focused business utility. Unlike generic SaaS platforms, every feature was purpose-built for the specific workflows, revenue models, and customer relationships of a fine art gallery.

The platform eliminates dependency on Artsy Pro ($500–$2,000/mo), third-party booking systems, email marketing tools, CRM software, and accounting add-ons — consolidating everything under a single, owned, extensible system.

At a replacement cost of $95,000–$145,000 and annual SaaS cost savings of $7,200–$26,400, the platform pays for itself within the first year of operation.
